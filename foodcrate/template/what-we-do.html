What we do



We campaign for a healthy and sustainable food system, which is publicly accountable and socially and environmentally responsible.



Our initiatives


Feeding us


If we change the way we farm and what we eat, we could improve our health, protect nature, combat climate change and be more food secure and affordable

Food Insecurity Tracking

We conduct regular surveys to assess food insecurity levels.
Foodcrate has been commissioning a series of surveys since the outbreak of the Covid pandemic in March 2020, to assess the impact of household food insecurity in Ghana. We track and report on the experiences of food insecurity, particularly focusing on specific groups such as families with children, ethnic groups, boarding schools, people with disabilities etc.

Fix-Our-Food

We are working on Fix-Our-Food, movement a research work led by kings worth farms. Fix-Our-Food has a vision to transform food systems towards being ‘regenerative’.
We are a key partner on the Fix-Our-Food in Schools. The team will be working with schools to transform food menus, environments, education and procurement. We aim to reduce inequalities in health and diet quality early in life and bring about dietary change more broadly. One example of this is our focus on improving food provision.

True cost of food

We pay for food in many ways, not just at checkout or food joint, but the true cost varies according to how food is produced and how this impacts our health and environment





Events

Foodcrate hosts its own event programme in addition to participating in partner events across multiple platforms.
These include campaign and report launches, policy panels, business and cities webinars.
We record and create films to share with global audiences to engage with our work and take action.
